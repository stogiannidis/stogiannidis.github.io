As we step into 2026, I find myself reflecting on the journey so far and the adventures that lie ahead.

## A New Chapter

Starting my PhD at The University of Edinburgh has been one of the most transformative experiences of my life. The academic environment, the brilliant colleagues, and the opportunity to dive deep into research on spatial and temporal reasoning in Foundation Models has been incredibly fulfilling.

## Lessons Learned

The first year has taught me several valuable lessons:

- **Patience is a virtue**: Research rarely goes as planned, and that's okay
- **Collaboration is key**: The best ideas often emerge from discussions with colleagues
- **Balance matters**: Taking care of mental health is as important as publishing papers

## Looking Forward

As I look ahead to 2026, I'm excited about the research directions we're exploring. The intersection of Computer Vision, NLP, and Robotics continues to present fascinating challenges and opportunities.

Stay tuned for more updates on my research journey!
